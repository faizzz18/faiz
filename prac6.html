APJ Abdul Kalam

Missile Man's Memories

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, fondly known as the Missile Man of India, was a visionary scientist, a dedicated teacher, and the 11th President of India. Born on October 15, 1931, in a small town of Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he rose from humble beginnings to become one of the most respected figures in Indian history. With a deep passion for science and education, he played a vital role in India’s space and defense programs, including the successful Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998. His journey from a newspaper boy to a celebrated scientist and leader is a true inspiration. Dr. Kalam believed in dreaming big and working hard. His love for children and students earned him the title People’s President. Even after his presidency, he continued to inspire the youth through lectures and books like Wings of Fire and Ignited Minds. He passed away doing what he loved most—teaching—on July 27, 2015. Though he is no longer with us, his ideals, wisdom, and dreams for a better India live on in every heart he touched. “Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action.” – Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Major Achivements

  • Led development of India's missiles (Agni & Prithvi)

  • Key figure in Pokhran-II nuclear tests (1998)

  • Directed launch of India’s first satellite vehicle (SLV-III)

  • Served as the 11th President of India (2002–2007)

  • Awarded Bharat Ratna, Padma Vibhushan, and Padma Bhushan

  • Inspired millions through education, books, and lectures
